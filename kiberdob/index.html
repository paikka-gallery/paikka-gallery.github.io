PAIKKA — KIBERDOB

What is Kiberdob and who we are?

Kiberdob which literally translates from Hungarian to English as ‘cyberdrum’ is a series of projects. The main objective of the project is to collectively create an interactive audio-visual device (named ‘kiberdob’) which will help anyone who interacts with it to experience a flow state.



There have been five projects implemented so far.

Kiberdob as an installation:

Kiberdob is a multiplayer electronic instrument designed to connect non-musicians with musicians and help them to experience the joy of improvisational and collaborative music creation. It is always site-specific, relies on a wide spectrum of triggers, such as its natural and/or artificial surroundings or human movements, and built with materials that are found around the area where Kiberdob settles.

Kiberdob as a foucauldian heterotopia:

In short, foucauldian heterotopias are places that are in contrast with every other place, as within the borders of these places a utopian dream is present for a limited amount of time. During the planning, the implementation and then the presentation of the project, the participants can experience living in a utopian community. Aspects of the utopian community are defined together by the participants through a series of workshops. The presentation is the last stage of the project as the creator community invites visitors to join their slowly dissolving heterotopia.

PAIKKA

which literally translates from Finnish to English as ‘place’ is an inclusive art collective which welcomes any creator (whether artist or not) to join their experimental projects that explore the topics, such as art, technology, and science. From 2021, PAIKKA occupied its first physical space in downtown Budapest and organises workshops, exhibitions, and retreats.
The goal of the collective is to create opportunities that art and educational institutes miss and to create a platform where creators can exchange skills and knowledge. The collective is run by volunteers who work in a non-hierarchical and always evolving system.

Project proposal for Maajaam

During the three weeks long residency program, 5 members of PAIKKA would build a Kiberdob on the Maajaam farm with the involvement of locals living in the surrounding settlements. The participant achievement would be achieved throughout a series of workshops which are aimed to generate discourse about the positive and negative aspects of technology on humans and nature. As at the moment of writing this application we do not know to which extent the participant involvement is possible, therefore, we would like to leave the process planning until we have more knowledge about it. 1. In case it is possible, we would hold four workshops and a final event with participants. A workshop would take a day (approximately 8 hours + breaks) and involve 15 participants. The four workshops would be thematized around the four elements: fire, water, earth, and air. Participants would be guided to focus on the theme of the workshops through various activities, such as performative group exercises, guided relaxation, facilitated brainstorming sessions, etc. The artists of PAIKKA function as facilitators who help the participants to define their concept and implement it.

2. In case organising four workshops with participants is not possible, we would create the four parts of the Kiberdob with a special focus to make it interactive. At the presentation of the installation, we would organise an opening and a closing ceremony. In between visitors could hike around the Maajaam farm and interact with the instrument. In either scenario, we would put high emphasis on experience design and interactivity in a way that participants/visitors will have the chance to form their own thoughts and engage in discussions with each other. The instrument would be built in a way that it is transportable and durational. We won’t need any new materials except some sensor modules as we always build the instrument respecting nature. We won’t produce waste.

What we need

Kiberdob is always built with whatever we bring (e.g.: beamers, microcomputers, sensors, DIY synths.) and with what we find at the spot where we build it. Since our project relies on participant inclusion, we need help with the following:
- Support for organising participants
- Help with branding
